About Re-Bound

From “extra pages” to powerful beginnings.

Every academic year ends with half-used notebooks. Re-Bound collects those unused pages, carefully sorts and binds them into fresh notebooks, and distributes them to children who need them the most. It’s simple, sustainable, and powered by students, families, and communities.

How it works

  1. Collect: Students, families, and offices drop their gently used notebooks at Re-Bound collection points.
  2. Sort: Volunteers separate blank pages and remove torn or scribbled sheets.
  3. Rebind: Pages are trimmed, grouped, and bound into sturdy, usable notebooks.
  4. Distribute: Partner NGOs, schools, and groups help us deliver them to children in need.

Why this matters

  • Reduces paper waste by turning “leftover” into “essential”.
  • Makes quality notebooks accessible to students who can’t always afford them.
  • Helps schools, homes, and workplaces build a culture of sustainability and empathy.
  • Shows how small, consistent actions can create measurable impact.

For schools, corporates & homes

Host a Re-Bound collection point

Re-Bound collection points can live in schools, corporate offices, apartment communities, volunteer homes - anywhere people use notebooks and want to give their unused pages a second life.

Here’s the simple starter kit:

  • 1. Choose a visible spot – reception, lobby, corridor, staff room, or community hall.
  • 2. Place a labelled box for people to drop their half-used notebooks or unused pages.
  • 3. Nominate a Re-Bound volunteer to keep an eye on the box and share updates.
  • 4. Sort & bundle pages every few weeks so they are ready for binding.
  • 5. Coordinate with us so we can help with binding support or connect you to distribution partners.

When you’re ready, use the “Count me in” button to share your details and mention that you’d like to host a collection point.
